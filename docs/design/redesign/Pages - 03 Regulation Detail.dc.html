Verified · checked against the source

MEPC.377(80) is a framework resolution — not directly operative convention text — binding IMO Member States (flag states, port states) as primary duty-bearers, not freight forwarders or shippers. Obligations flow downward through MARPOL Annex VI amendments (EEXI, CII, SEEMP), enforced against shipowners, operators, and managers of vessels ≥400 GT on international voyages.

Our analysis

Tier-1 obligated parties: vessel owners/operators and ship managers — they hold SEEMP, EEXI certification, and CII rating obligations. Tier-2 contractual chain (workspace position): the workspace is a cargo interest, not a "ship operator" — but GHG levy and fuel-standard costs will pass through freight rates; the workspace is a cost-absorber. Tier-3 reporting interface: where clients face scope 3 obligations (CSRD, SEC), the workspace becomes a data intermediary supplying per-shipment GHG intensity from carriers' CII and DCS reports.

Our analysis · confirm with counsel

Whether the workspace, acting as a contractual carrier (issuing its own house bill of lading for ocean legs), assumes any MARPOL-adjacent responsibility for fuel documentation should be confirmed with maritime law counsel per jurisdiction (US, EU, Singapore, Hong Kong).
Verified

No EPR-style registration falls on the workspace. Under MARPOL Annex VI, ships ≥5,000 GT report annual fuel consumption through the IMO DCS — submitted by the shipowner/operator to the flag state, then to IMO GISIS. The obligation rests with the carrier.

Our analysis · what to collect

Per ocean carrier: (a) vessel IMO number; (b) voyage CII rating; (c) fuel type and consumption per voyage or TEU/tonne-mile; (d) the carrier's annual DCS-derived Statement of Compliance. CII ratings appear on the vessel's SEEMP Part III.

Promised but pending at the IMO

Expanded DCS data granularity (per-voyage/per-route emissions) was discussed at MEPC 80 and deferred; it remains under intersessional deliberation and is not yet a published reporting format.

Tier 1 · Directly bound

Cost-absorber in the contractual chain: GHG levy and fuel-standard costs pass through freight rates. Must understand how carriers disclose and invoice these costs.

Data intermediary for shipper-clients with scope 3 obligations (CSRD, SEC) — supplies per-shipment GHG intensity from carrier CII/DCS reports.

Penalty schedule

No direct penalty schedule · by design

MEPC.377(80) is a framework strategy resolution — it carries no penalty provisions of its own. Enforcement flows through MARPOL Annex VI instruments (EEXI, CII, SEEMP) applied by flag and port states against vessel owners and operators. Penalties relevant to the workspace arrive indirectly, as carrier compliance costs passed through freight rates — tracked under Exposure.
