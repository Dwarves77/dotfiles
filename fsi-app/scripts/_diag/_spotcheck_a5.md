# ORIGINAL
# Sustainable Air Freight Alliance (SAFA)

**Format:** Market Signal Brief | **Severity:** MONITORING | **Item type:** initiative

---

## Section 1: What's Moving and What Triggered It

SAFA — the Sustainable Air Freight Alliance — is a buyer-supplier collaboration platform, conveyed by BSR (Business for Social Responsibility), designed to enable shippers, freight forwarders, and air freight carriers to jointly track and reduce carbon dioxide emissions from air freight and promote responsible freight transport. *Source: "Improve Sustainability with New Air Freight Alliance," BSR, February 5, 2019. https://www.bsr.org/en/blog/improve-sustainability-with-new-air-freight-alliance.

The initiative was launched publicly on February 5, 2019, following an incubation period with active contribution from over 15 companies. *Source: "Improve Sustainability with New Air Freight Alliance," BSR, February 5, 2019. https://www.bsr.org/en/blog/improve-sustainability-with-new-air-freight-alliance.

Founding participants named at launch include DB Schenker, DHL, Finnair, GEODIS, H&M, Maersk, Nike, and United Airlines. *Source: "Improve Sustainability with New Air Freight Alliance," BSR, February 5, 2019. https://www.bsr.org/en/blog/improve-sustainability-with-new-air-freight-alliance. Membership has since expanded; Delta Cargo's subsequent SAFA membership has been reported by Air Cargo News. *Source: "Delta Cargo Joins Sustainable Air Freight Alliance," Air Cargo News. https://www.aircargonews.net/policy/environment/delta-cargo-joins-sustainable-air-freight-alliance/. [Note: The Air Cargo News source page returned a Cloudflare verification block during retrieval; Delta Cargo membership is cited from the corroborating source description provided and flagged for verification against the full article.]

Two structural triggers drove SAFA's formation:

- **The sector growth imperative.** Air transport represents around two percent of global carbon dioxide emissions. The air freight sector is projected to grow at five percent per year until 2050 — faster than any other mode — with potential to increase emissions significantly. *Source: "Improve Sustainability with New Air Freight Alliance," BSR, February 5, 2019. https://www.bsr.org/en/blog/improve-sustainability-with-new-air-freight-alliance.

- **The data-transparency gap.** Prior to SAFA, there was no consistent forum for direct business-to-business exchange between stakeholders, and a general lack of transparency around the application and use of the environmental performance information requested from airlines by shippers for their reporting and decision-making needs. *Source: "Improve Sustainability with New Air Freight Alliance," BSR, February 5, 2019. https://www.bsr.org/en/blog/improve-sustainability-with-new-air-freight-alliance.

*Operational implication:* For workspaces operating as freight forwarders with air as the primary transport mode, SAFA directly addresses the data gap that currently sits between carrier-level emissions performance and shipper-facing Scope 3 reporting — the structural friction point that clients increasingly flag in sustainability questionnaires and tender requirements.

## Section 2: Who's Driving It and What They Want

**BSR (convener/facilitator):** BSR functions as the neutral third-party convener and secretariat. Its stated role is to provide a secure, assured, and confidential third-party reporting platform, and to help shape international standards in line with ICAO and IATA's work. *Source: "Sustainable Air Freight Alliance: Introductory Webinar," BSR, February 27, 2019. https://www.bsr.org/en/events/sustainable-air-freight-alliance-webinar.

**Freight forwarders (DB Schenker, DHL, GEODIS):** These participants seek standardised, credible emissions data from carriers that they can pass through to shipper clients. *Analytical inference:* Forwarders face increasing client demand for flight-level or route-level carbon data to satisfy their own Scope 3 reporting obligations, and a standardised industry methodology reduces the cost and reputational risk of maintaining bespoke carrier-by-carrier data collection.

**Carriers (Finnair, United Airlines, and reported Delta Cargo):** Airlines seek a coordinated engagement channel with large corporate shippers and forwarders, allowing them to demonstrate environmental performance without asymmetric bilateral disclosure requirements from each buyer individually.

**Shippers (H&M, Nike, Maersk):** Large cargo shippers with public science-based targets require verified, standardised air freight emissions data to populate their Scope 3 Category 4 (upstream transportation) disclosures. *Analytical inference:* For consumer-brand shippers subject to the EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) or SEC climate disclosure rules, the absence of a standard methodology creates reporting liability that SAFA is positioned to reduce.

**Smart Freight Centre (standards alignment):** SAFA has aligned its measurement framework with work coordinated through the Global Logistics Emissions Council (GLEC) in coordination with IATA. *Source: "Improve Sustainability with New Air Freight Alliance," BSR, February 5, 2019. https://www.bsr.org/en/blog/improve-sustainability-with-new-air-freight-alliance.

## Section 3: Expected Trajectory and Conversion Triggers

**Current phase and status:** SAFA is an active, membership-open multi-stakeholder initiative. It is not a regulation; membership is voluntary. The initiative has expanded beyond its founding eight named participants (Delta Cargo has been reported as a subsequent member). No published membership count or total figure has been identified in the source blocks as of this brief's generation date.

*Specific current member count not available from primary sources as of 2026-06-10.*

**SAF as the primary decarbonisation pathway:** BSR's published analysis identifies SAF as the most promising decarbonisation pathway in the near term for aviation, with ASTM International having approved seven SAF production pathways that can produce drop-in SAF. *Source: "Sustainable Aviation Fuels Take Flight," BSR. https://www.bsr.org/en/emerging-issues/sustainable-aviation-fuels-take-flight.

When used pure and unblended, SAF can help reduce the lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions from aviation by as much as 80% relative to conventional fuel. *Source: "Mitigating emissions from air freight: Unlocking the potential of SAF with book and claim," MIT Technology Review, January 12, 2026. https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1128939/mitigating-emissions-from-air-freight-unlocking-the-potential-of-saf-with-book-and-claim.

**Book-and-claim as the access mechanism:** Book and claim is a chain of custody model where the flow of administrative records is not necessarily connected to the physical product through the supply chain (source: ISO 22095:2020). *Source: "Mitigating emissions from air freight: Unlocking the potential of SAF with book and claim," MIT Technology Review, January 12, 2026. https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1128939/mitigating-emissions-from-air-freight-unlocking-the-potential-of-saf-with-book-and-claim.

Book and claim is not yet accepted by the Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Protocol nor the Science Based Targets Initiative (SBTi) as a recognised model for reducing greenhouse gas emissions — though there are hopes that might change. *Source: "Mitigating emissions from air freight: Unlocking the potential of SAF with book and claim," MIT Technology Review, January 12, 2026. https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1128939/mitigating-emissions-from-air-freight-unlocking-the-potential-of-saf-with-book-and-claim.

*Analytical inference:* GHG Protocol and SBTi recognition of book-and-claim is the single most significant conversion trigger for SAFA-aligned SAF procurement. Until that recognition lands, SAF certificates purchased through book-and-claim cannot be applied to reduce the Scope 3 emissions figure that clients and regulators will scrutinise — limiting the mechanism to forward-positioning value rather than immediate compliance credit.

The Smart Freight Centre is closely involved in technical working groups looking to build a system where, in addition to physical inventory, market-based inventories are also pursued. *Source: "Mitigating emissions from air freight: Unlocking the potential of SAF with book and claim," MIT Technology Review, January 12, 2026. https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1128939/mitigating-emissions-from-air-freight-unlocking-the-potential-of-saf-with-book-and-claim.

*Specific GHG Protocol or SBTi recognition decision date not available from primary sources as of 2026-06-10.*

**Regulatory accelerants already in place:** The European Commission's ReFuelEU Aviation plan proposes a 2 percent SAF mandate by 2025, 5 percent by 2030, and 63 percent by 2050. *Source: "Sustainable Aviation Fuels Take Flight," BSR. https://www.bsr.org/en/emerging-issues/sustainable-aviation-fuels-take-flight. Scope 3 disclosure obligations under CSRD and proposed SEC climate rules independently elevate the commercial urgency of having credible, standardised air freight emissions data — which is precisely what SAFA provides. *Source: "Sustainable Aviation Fuels Take Flight," BSR. https://www.bsr.org/en/emerging-issues/sustainable-aviation-fuels-take-flight.

**Re-check window — MONITORING:** The outcome of GHG Protocol and SBTi engagement on book-and-claim recognition is the pending event that most changes SAFA's operational utility. *Analytical inference:* Based on stated Smart Freight Centre involvement in technical working groups (sourced above), a re-check of published GHG Protocol and SBTi guidance on book-and-claim is warranted at Q4 2026, aligned with the typical 12–18 month standards-revision cycle for working group outputs of this type.

## Section 4: Operational and Cost Implications If It Materialises

**Air-primary workspaces — primary exposure:**

Emissions from air freight have increased by 25% since 2019, according to a 2024 analysis by environmental advocacy organisation Stand.Earth, yielding 93.8 million tonnes from air freight overall. *Source: "Mitigating emissions from air freight: Unlocking the potential of SAF with book and claim," MIT Technology Review, January 12, 2026. https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1128939/mitigating-emissions-from-air-freight-unlocking-the-potential-of-saf-with-book-and-claim.

*Operational implication:* For workspaces for which air is the primary transport mode — covering live events, fine art, luxury goods, film and TV production, high-value automotive, and humanitarian verticals where speed and security requirements make air non-negotiable — this emissions trajectory translates directly into growing Scope 3 disclosure liability for clients and a tightening commercial expectation that their forwarder can provide route-level carbon data.

**SAF cost premium:** SAF currently costs at least twice the price of conventional jet fuel, making cost a significant barrier hindering broader adoption. *Source: "Mitigating emissions from air freight: Unlocking the potential of SAF with book and claim," MIT Technology Review, January 12, 2026. https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1128939/mitigating-emissions-from-air-freight-unlocking-the-potential-of-saf-with-book-and-claim.

*Operational implication:* SAF surcharges, when passed through carrier commercial structures to forwarders and then to shippers, represent a COST ALERT for air-dependent verticals. The timing and magnitude of pass-through rates are not fixed in the sourced material and will vary by carrier, route, and contract structure.

*Specific SAF surcharge rates by carrier or route not available from primary sources as of 2026-06-10.*

**Book-and-claim access mechanics:** SAF production outlets have increased from fewer than 20 locations in 2021 to 114 as of April 2025, according to sustainability solutions framework 4Air, accounting for only 92 airports worldwide out of more than 40,000. *Source: "Mitigating emissions from air freight: Unlocking the potential of SAF with book and claim," MIT Technology Review, January 12, 2026. https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1128939/mitigating-emissions-from-air-freight-unlocking-the-potential-of-saf-with-book-and-claim.

*Operational implication:* Because physical SAF availability at the airports most relevant to the workspace's trade lanes (Americas, Europe, Asia) is uneven, book-and-claim is likely the only mechanism that would allow the workspace to offer clients SAF-attributed shipments at scale across its route network in the near term. This is both the mechanism's value and its current limitation (GHG Protocol non-recognition noted above).

**The DB Schenker / Lufthansa Cargo precedent:** DB Schenker, a named SAFA founding member, has confirmed active membership and describes a SAF pioneer project with Lufthansa Cargo on the Frankfurt–Shanghai (FRA–PVG) route. *Source: "Sustainability in Air Freight," DB Schenker. https://www.dbschenker.com/global/products/air-freight/sustainability-in-air-freight. [Note: The DB Schenker source page returned navigation/cookie-consent content rather than body text; the FRA–PVG programme detail is drawn from the corroborating source description provided for this URL and is flagged for verification against the live page.]

*Analytical inference:* For workspaces serving trans-Eurasian lanes (Europe–Asia being among the highest-density air freight corridors), the FRA–PVG programme demonstrates that SAFA-affiliated carriers are already operating SAF-attributed routes that a forwarder member could use to substantiate client-facing emissions reduction claims, subject to the book-and-claim recognition caveat noted above.

## Section 5: Competitive Implications

*Analytical inference:* SAFA membership creates a structural information advantage: members receive standardised, carrier-reported emissions data through a neutral BSR-administered platform, while non-members rely on bilateral carrier disclosures of variable quality and comparability. For a freight forwarder operating across multiple carriers and trade lanes, the difference in data quality is material when responding to client RFPs with Scope 3 disclosure requirements.

*Analytical inference:* Among the named founding forwarders — DB Schenker and GEODIS — both are direct competitors to a workspace operating in the general freight forwarding market. DHL is a named founding member and subsequent major public advocate of book-and-claim (as cited in the MIT Technology Review source). These three forwarders have multi-year head starts in SAFA's methodology, client reporting cadence, and carrier data relationships.

*Analytical inference:* For verticals such as live events, luxury goods, and film and TV production — where the workspace's clients are typically large brand-owners or production companies with their own sustainability reporting obligations — the ability to provide SAFA-aligned, methodology-consistent air freight emissions data is increasingly a commercial differentiator in tender evaluation.

*Analytical inference:* Workspaces that join SAFA now gain access to the collaborative standard-shaping process (the platform allows members to help shape international standards, in line with ICAO, IATA's work, and sectorial initiatives) before those standards are finalised — a COMPETITIVE EDGE that closes as standards solidify.

*Source for SAFA standard-shaping language:* "Sustainable Air Freight Alliance: Introductory Webinar," BSR, February 27, 2019. https://www.bsr.org/en/events/sustainable-air-freight-alliance-webinar.

## Section 6: Client Conversation Talking Points

**What the workspace can credibly say:**

- "Air freight emissions from the sector have grown by 25% since 2019. We are positioning to give clients route-level carbon data through an industry-standard methodology aligned with ICAO and IATA frameworks, rather than estimates." *(Grounds to sourced MIT Technology Review / BSR data above.)*
- "SAF is the primary near-term decarbonisation pathway for aviation, and it can reduce lifecycle GHG emissions by up to 80% when used pure. We are monitoring the book-and-claim access mechanism and its regulatory recognition trajectory." *(Grounds to MIT Technology Review and BSR SAF sources above.)*
- "The EU's ReFuelEU Aviation plan sets a 2% SAF mandate from 2025 and 5% by 2030. This will drive carrier cost structures, and we are tracking how those costs are passed through to freight clients." *(Grounds to BSR SAF source above.)*

**Pitfalls to avoid:**

- Do not claim that book-and-claim SAF certificates reduce your clients' Scope 3 emissions under current GHG Protocol or SBTi rules. This is explicitly not the case as of the sourced material. The correct framing is "forward-positioned" or "supportive of SAF scale-up" — not "Scope 3 compliant."
- Do not claim SAFA membership or methodology alignment unless the workspace has completed onboarding. The platform uses a third-party reporting mechanism; self-reporting as SAFA-aligned without membership is an overclaim.
- Do not present SAF surcharge cost impact to clients as certain or quantified until carrier-specific data is available. The "at least twice the price" figure applies to SAF itself; freight-level surcharge rates vary.

**Questions to pose to clients:**

- "Does your sustainability framework or investor reporting currently require route-level air freight emissions data, or is a modal average sufficient for this cycle?"
- "Are you currently required to report Scope 3 Category 4 under CSRD or an equivalent framework? If so, what methodology have your auditors accepted for air freight?"
- "Have any of your other forwarders provided SAFA-aligned emissions data? We are evaluating the same framework."

## Section 7: What the Workspace Should Do Now

**1. Initiate SAFA membership evaluation — COMPETITIVE EDGE | Within 30 days**

Contact BSR directly to obtain current membership terms, fee structure, and onboarding timeline. The alliance is described as open for membership. *Source: "Improve Sustainability with New Air Freight Alliance," BSR, February 5, 2019. https://www.bsr.org/en/blog/improve-sustainability-with-new-air-freight-alliance. Membership grants access to the neutral third-party reporting platform, standardised carrier emissions data, and the standard-shaping working process — all of which require active participation to access.

**2. Map carrier relationships against SAFA membership — MONITORING | Within 60 days**

Identify which carriers the workspace routes air freight through across its Americas, Europe, and Asia trade lanes. Cross-reference against confirmed SAFA carrier members (Finnair, United Airlines, and reported Delta Cargo are named in sources). Carriers outside SAFA do not supply standardised SAFA-methodology emissions data; this gap needs to be quantified before client disclosure commitments are made.

**3. Audit current air freight emissions data quality — ACTION REQUIRED for workspaces with active CSRD-adjacent client accounts | Within 60 days**

Determine whether the workspace is currently receiving flight-level or route-level emissions data from carriers, or whether it relies on modal-average estimates. SAFA's core value proposition is replacing estimates with standardised, carrier-reported, third-party-assured data. The gap between current state and SAFA-standard data is the workspace's current client disclosure liability.

**4. Add book-and-claim clause evaluation to SAF vendor conversations — MONITORING**

If the workspace is already in dialogue with SAF suppliers or carriers about SAF surcharge products, confirm whether those products are backed by a recognised book-and-claim platform (such as Shell's Avelia, referenced in the MIT Technology Review source). Avelia expanded in 2024 to encompass air freight in addition to business travel. *Source: "Mitigating emissions from air freight: Unlocking the potential of SAF with book and claim," MIT Technology Review, January 12, 2026. https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1128939/mitigating-emissions-from-air-freight-unlocking-the-potential-of-saf-with-book-and-claim. *Legal Confirmation Required:* whether book-and-claim certificates purchased through any specific platform satisfy current contractual Scope 3 commitments in existing client agreements requires counsel and auditor review per client account.

**5. Track GHG Protocol and SBTi book-and-claim recognition — MONITORING | Re-check Q4 2026**

*Analytical inference:* The Smart Freight Centre's confirmed involvement in technical working groups on book-and-claim recognition (sourced above) suggests a Q4 2026 window as the earliest point at which updated GHG Protocol or SBTi guidance could materially change the compliance value of book-and-claim SAF procurement. Brief the operations and commercial teams at that point to reassess whether SAF surcharge products can be presented to clients as Scope 3 credit-generating tools.
