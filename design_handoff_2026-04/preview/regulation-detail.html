The EU has tightened the rules for how much carbon-heavy freight can cross its borders without paying a tax. Specifically, the threshold drops from 50,000 kg to 2,500 kg. For your art-handling business that means most international shipments now require verified emissions data — or you pay 20% extra in penalties. The deadline is 1 July 2026.

What changed

The Commission has finalised the delegated act amending Regulation (EU) 2023/956. The de-minimis threshold for embedded emissions reporting drops from 50,000 kg to 2,500 kg per consignment, effective 1 July 2026.

This is significant for specialty freight categories — including fine-art transport, scientific instruments, and high-value luxury goods — that previously fell below the threshold by weight but now sit clearly within scope. The Commission's impact assessment estimates an additional 168,000 consignments per year will require verified Embedded Emissions Reports (EERs).

Why it matters

  • CostAn estimated €2.4M annualised increase in CBAM levies across your 17 affected lanes at current shipment volumes.
  • OpsVerifier capacity is constrained — published wait times have risen to 9–11 weeks at three of the five major EU verifier hubs.
  • ClientTier-1 shippers will request verified declarations from 1 July; default values invite uplift charges that flow back to the customer.
  • ComplianceFailure to file a verified EER within 30 days of import incurs a 50 EUR/tCO₂e administrative penalty on top of the levy.
