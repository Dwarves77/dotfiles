CBAM Article 30 delegated act narrows Article 7(1) exemption to cargo under 2,500 kg

The Commission's 14 October 2026 delegated act removes the small-shipment carve-out for chartered air freight above the new threshold. Workspaces moving high-value cargo via charter file quarterly returns from 1 April 2027.

✦ Short summary

Generated · 30-second read

CBAM Article 30 (the delegated act of 14 Oct 2026) narrows the Article 7(1) exemption so charter air freight over 2,500 kg gross (including packaging) now triggers full CBAM obligations: declarant registration, quarterly emissions returns, third-party verification, and certificate purchase. For workspaces commissioning art-tour and live-events charters from EU origins above this threshold, the obligation lands 1 April 2027 with first return due 30 April 2027.

Operational briefing

6 segments · ~3-minute read
1What it is

EU Commission delegated act narrowing CBAM Article 7(1)

A delegated act of 14 October 2026 amending CBAM Regulation 2023/956. Removes the small-shipment carve-out for chartered air freight by lowering the gross-weight threshold to 2,500 kg (including packaging weight).

Source: OJ L 287/14 · EUR-Lex

2What it requires

Five obligations on EU-bound charter movements >2,500 kg

  • Declarant registration on EU CBAM Registry
  • Quarterly emissions return per consignment
  • Embedded-emissions data retention (5 years)
  • Certificate purchase for actual emissions
  • Annual third-party verification (Annex II accredited)
3Who it affects

Workspaces commissioning charter air freight

Two roles per transaction: Importer of record on own-account charter (~60% of EU-bound volume) and Indirect importer when commissioning on behalf of a named client (~40%). The Article 30(2)(b) language on indirect-importer obligation transfer is ambiguous — legal confirmation required.

4What it costs

€20 / tCO₂e + 100% surcharge for missing returns

Late or default-value returns trigger the penalty rate. Material on every quarter for art-tour and live-events movements that routinely cross the threshold. Certificate purchase priced annually based on actual emissions; budget Q3 2027 forward.

5What to do

Three immediate-action items, dated

  • 30 days · spec booking-system capture (HS code, gross mass, declared CO₂e per consignment)
  • 60 days · reconfirm role placement with legal counsel
  • By 31 Mar 2027 · open CBAM declarant registration on EU Registry
6Open questions

What the workspace does not yet know

  • Indirect-importer filing obligation transfer (CCC resolution deferred to Q1 2027)
  • Verifier selection (RFP not yet opened)
  • Certificate pricing posture (DG TAXUD pricing schedule pending)
  • Booking-system capture implementation timeline

✦ Why it matters

Charter air freight has historically been exempt from CBAM under the small-shipment carve-out. This act eliminates the carve-out for cargo above 2,500 kg gross. The art and live-events verticals routinely operate at this scale — a single art tour or stage-rigging movement crosses the threshold. The €20/tCO₂e penalty plus 100% surcharge for missing returns is material on every quarter.

The Customs Code Committee's deferral of the indirect-importer question creates a 6-month window where the workspace's role placement is uncertain. Counsel must confirm whether filings transfer to clients or remain with the commissioning party before Q2 2027 close.

§4How the workspace sits in the compliance chainAlways

The roles the regulation defines, which ones the workspace occupies per transaction type, and where legal confirmation is required.

The act defines four roles in the import chain. The workspace occupies two of them, distinct per transaction type.

Importer of record — when chartering air freight on the workspace's own account. Filing obligation falls on the workspace directly. ~60% of EU-bound charter volume per booking history.

Indirect importer — when commissioning charter on behalf of a named client. Article 30(2)(b) is ambiguous on whether the filing obligation transfers automatically or requires explicit contractual assignment. Legal Confirmation Required.

The Manufacturer-Producer and Distributor roles defined by the act do not apply to the workspace's transaction set.

Source: Delegated act Articles 30, 26(3), 7(1).
